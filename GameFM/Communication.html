Đề Xuất
Posted: July 18
Honestly, this DLC completely revitalized the base game for me. The ability to colonize underwater regions adds a whole new layer of strategy and immersion. Watching my colony slowly adapt to deep-sea pressures and learning to manage oxygen flow, power, and aquatic creatures was just so engaging. The atmosphere alone makes the entire experience unforgettable. Playing with the underwater biome introduces new challenges that break the routine of land-based colonies. You now have to consider pressure resistance for structures, and your colonists need specialized suits to even step outside. The game forces you to rethink how you manage space, resources, and time. It’s no longer about just farming and building – it’s about survival under extreme conditions. At first, I struggled to adjust to the slower pacing. You can’t just expand like usual – you need to gradually reinforce your base, unlock pressure-resistant tech, and deal with strange new threats like trench sirens or electrical storms. But that slow ramp-up makes the payoff even better. Once your systems are in place, it becomes a smooth, almost meditative loop of maintenance, exploration, and progress. One thing I appreciated most was how this DLC doesn't just add fluff – it meaningfully changes how you play. The new tech tree, unique fauna, and underwater trade routes all combine to create something truly fresh. It’s not perfect – some UI elements could use improvement – but overall, it’s one of the most creative expansions I’ve played in a long time.
